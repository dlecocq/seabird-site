Indexing Plate 01 Dec 2013

I found myself in need of an indexing plate for a project recently. Since my lathe isn't itself equipped with one, I proceeded to make one.

This design is not my own, but at this point I'm not sure where I saw it first. Certainly from someone smarter than me. The idea is to take a piece of 1/4" plywood that can sit on the threading of the headstock and behind the chuck. Evenly-spaced notches in the edge of the plate lock into a wedge that slides in the lathe bed, allowing us to easily keep the headstock from spinning freely.

Rather than trying to construct the even spacings, I printed off a degree wheel that I found on the internet at about 8 inches. If you plan to make multiple indexing plates, it's a good idea to print out a few of these and make them all at once to ensure they're all roughly the same size.

After applying the degree wheel to a piece of plywood with some spray adhesive, cut out the degree wheel keeping it slightly oversized. Then drill out the center with a whole that can accommodate the headstock spindle, and secure it with the lathe chuck and true it up. Taking it back off the lathe, I made triangular notches of the same size evenly spaced around the edge. Of course, this will involve a little bit of math to make sure you end up with the right number of positions.

For the wedge I made it out of a scrap piece of 2x4, making sure to angle the wedge so that it could fit all of my indexing plates if they were slighly different sizes. It's a little bit difficult to just describe, so perhaps a picture here will serve best.
