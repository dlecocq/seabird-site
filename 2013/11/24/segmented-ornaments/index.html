Segmented Tree Ornaments 24 Nov 2013

Coming off of the successful completion of the snowflake ornaments, I decided to take on some of my first segmented turning projects. The always great Frank Howarth made a segmented Christmas ornament with some Christmas-tree shapes embedded in it. Though beautiful, I thought it was sort of a shame that it took so much wood and segmented work for a single ornament.

I figured that instead, I could take the completed segments and turn the log round and then slice it up. In the above video, Frank completed the design in two halves, but I decided to make each layer of the tree a single piece rather than two. Once cut, I glued each layer up and then sanded it flat once dried.

When gluing the layers together, Frank mentioned having trouble keeping the pieces aligned while clamping. In order to solve this, I took an old, snapped bandsaw blade and glued a couple of pieces of it into some wooden blocks, allowing the smooth backend of the blade to protrude by about 1/8". The plan was then to then cut shallow grooves into the center of each end of each layer and then allow the bandsaw blade to sit in the groove and keep the layers aligned. Turns out, it worked great. And important part of this approach, though is to make sure that each layer is nearly exactly the same length.

With the log all glued up, I mounted it on the lathe and turned it round. The log turned out to be about 4" in diameter, and more than a little dangerous to cut on the bandsaw as-is. To make it more stable, I glued a square block of wood to the end that I was holding to keep it from twisting around.

They took a fair amount of work (mostly the sanding), but in the end I made about 70 and they came out pretty well. I finished them with a polyurethane, but in hindsight I should have used some sanding sealer first.
