Snowflake Ornaments 22 Nov 2013

With a craft show coming up, and freshly inspired by Woodworking for Mere Mortals, I gave this project a shot. The vast majority of my woodworking has been turning, but the project looked relatively accessible, and indeed it was. It also gave me a chance to visit local lumber yard, Alki Lumber, for the first time.

Looking to experiment with designs a little bit, I tried making a few in paper first, but ultimately I found it to be not very helpful. Most of the designs were precluded as possibilities by my abilities with the router table.

The project when forth mostly as described in the Mere Mortals videos, though I ended up using my recently-made sanding disks to finish them rather than using a palm sander. And while I did make some using normal wood glue, I found them too difficult to clamp reliably that way, so I opted instead for some thick CA glue and holding them for about 30 seconds while they cured. This also allowed me to get through the project more quickly.

I also found it helpful to use the segmented turnings approach here and glue up each of the two halves of the snoflake log first, then sand them flat before gluing them together. Perhaps this was my inability to get perfect cuts on the table saw, but gluing up all six pieces at once didn't work well for me.

All in all, I made about 150 ornaments. Some of them will be sold at the craft fair, and some will be given out to friends and family and some more will grace our tree.
