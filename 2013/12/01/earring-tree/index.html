Earring Tree 01 Dec 2013

I was recently spending a lot of time preparing for an upcoming craft fair, including making a lot of turned earrings. I wanted a way to display these earrings and after scouring Etsy for some inspiration, I decided to make a tree out of wire with a wooden base and many curly branches from which to hang earrings.

It would begin with a wooden base that would hold 20 long aluminum wires, and I wanted all of these wires to be consistently spaced in a circle. I didn't trust measuring them out onto the base by hand, and so I ended up making an indexing plate. After turning the base, I marked a circle lightly in pencil and then used the indexing plate to make 20 evenly-spaced marks.

With these marks in place, I drilled some pilot holes by hand with a set of micro drill bits in a pin vise, and then drilled them at a size appropriate for the gauge of the wires and secured them in place with CA glue. In hindsight, I would have probably drilled the holes all the way through the base and secured the wires with epoxy or crimped the ends once through the hole.

In order to twist up all the wires into the trunk, I turned a second piece of wood similar to the base (all the evenly-spaced holes and all that). In this piece, the holes need to be large enough to allow the wires to pass through easily and without any resistance. There also needs to be a large hole in the center to hold the wires that as they get removed from the trunk to make the branches.

The twisting of the trunk begins with putting each wire through the corresponding hole in the winding tool. It can be a little tricky to wind the trunk evenly, but it gets easier as it progresses. Rather than just removing pull out wires to form the branches, it's best to pick a few wires to remove and place them temporarily in the hole in the middle of the tool, winding the remaining wires a bit and then pull them out to be branches. This way the branches can't simply unwind from the tree if they're pulled.

With the branches poking out from the main trunk, I found that twisting the branches into random patterns didn't work very well. Instead I wrapped each branch around a dowel repeatedly to form a coil. When all the branches have been made into coils, I pulled each branch to stretch out the coil revealing a number of nice and even places to hang ornaments. Fluff as needed.
